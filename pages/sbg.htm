Standards-Based Grading

This is a concise explanation of how grades are calculated under Prairie High School's new Standards-Based system.

  1. Each assignment is put into one or more bins by topic. These bins are called Standards.
  2. Each assignment is worth twice as much as the last, meaning that your final in a given class will be worth 50% of each Standard it covers.
  3. Each Standard factors into your final grade using these rules:
    • Key:
      • E = Exemplary (4)
      • M = Meeting (3)
      • APP = Approaching (2)
      • BG = Beginning (1)
      • NE = Not Evident (0)
      • NA = Not Assessed
    • To get an A: you need at least one E, and all the rest Ms, no APPs, BGs, or NEs.
    • To get a B: you need all Ms, no more than one APP, no BGs or NEs.
    • To get a C: you need all Ms or APPs, and no BGs.
    • To get a D: you need at least one APP, and all the rest BGs.
    • To get an F: you need all BGs and no NEs.
